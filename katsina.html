Katsina State

Katsina State, one of the historic Hausa Bakwai, was created in 1987 from the former Kaduna State. The capital city, Katsina, is famous for Islamic scholarship, trade, and historic landmarks such as the Gobarau Minaret and the Emir’s Palace.

Predominantly Hausa-Fulani, Katsina State is rich in culture and tradition, with agriculture as the backbone of its economy. The state remains an educational and cultural hub of Northern Nigeria.
